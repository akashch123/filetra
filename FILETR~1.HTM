Filetra vs WeTransfer vs Google Drive: Which Should You Use?

Filetra Blog · July 2026

Not all file sharing works the same way. WeTransfer and Google Drive are cloud-relay services: your file is uploaded to their servers first. Filetra is peer-to-peer: your file goes directly from your browser to the recipient's. That one architectural difference explains almost every practical difference between them.

Size limits

WeTransfer allows 2 GB per transfer on the free plan. Google Drive shares whatever fits in your storage quota — 15 GB free, shared with Gmail and Photos, so in practice much less. Filetra has no size limit, because there is no server storing your file. If your connection can carry it, you can send it.

Speed

Cloud services need two full trips: you upload at your upload speed, then the recipient downloads. With direct P2P transfer there is one trip, and it starts the moment the recipient opens the link — no waiting for an upload to finish before you can even share the link. For a 5 GB file on a typical home connection, that difference is often measured in tens of minutes.

Privacy

With cloud services, a copy of your file sits on someone else's server — for days on WeTransfer, indefinitely on Drive until you delete it. Shared Drive links are easy to forget and keep working long after you've moved on. With peer-to-peer transfer, no copy ever exists anywhere except your device and the recipient's. Close the tab and the link is dead.

Convenience

Cloud storage wins in one scenario: asynchronous sharing. If your recipient will download the file tomorrow while your laptop is off, you need a server to hold it. P2P requires both sides to be online at the same time — that's the honest trade-off.

Accounts and cost

Google Drive requires a Google account for the sender. WeTransfer's free tier is ad-supported with the 2 GB cap; larger transfers require a paid plan. Filetra needs no account and has no paid tier to upsell — direct transfer costs nothing to provide beyond a tiny connection handshake.

The bottom line

  • Choose Google Drive when the file should live somewhere long-term and be re-downloaded.
  • Choose WeTransfer when the recipient downloads later and the file is under 2 GB.
  • Choose Filetra when the file is big, the moment is now, or you don't want any copy left on a server. See how to send large files without the cloud.
