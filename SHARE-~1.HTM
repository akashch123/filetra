How to Share Files with No Size Limit: A Step-by-Step Guide

Filetra Blog · July 2026

File size limits exist because someone has to pay to store your file. Remove the storage, and the limit disappears with it. This guide walks through sharing a file of any size — 4K video, a game build, a dataset — directly from your browser, for free, without creating an account.

What you need

  • A modern browser (Chrome, Edge, Firefox, Safari — desktop or mobile).
  • An internet connection on both ends.
  • Nothing else. No app, no extension, no sign-up.

Step 1 — Open Filetra and pick your file

Go to the Filetra homepage and drag your file onto the drop zone, or click it to browse. The file is not uploaded anywhere — the page simply prepares it for direct streaming from your device.

Step 2 — Copy your share link

A unique link appears within a second or two. Hit Copy and send it to your recipient however you like: WhatsApp, Slack, email, SMS. The link points at your live browser session, not at a stored file.

Step 3 — Keep the tab open while they download

When your recipient opens the link, their browser connects directly to yours and shows the file name and size. The moment they click Download, the transfer streams across with a live progress bar and speed indicator on both ends. When it hits 100%, the file is saved on their device — and no copy exists anywhere in between.

Tips for very large transfers

  • Prefer Wi-Fi or ethernet over mobile data for multi-gigabyte files.
  • Stop the device from sleeping. A sleeping laptop pauses the transfer.
  • Zip folders first. Browsers transfer single files, so compress a folder into one .zip before sharing.
  • Coordinate timing. Both tabs must be open at the same time — a quick "opening it now" message avoids waiting.

Frequently asked questions

Is there really no size limit?

There is no imposed limit. Very large transfers are bounded only by your connection speed and how long both tabs stay open.

Can more than one person download?

Each share link connects your tab to one receiver session. For multiple people, share again after each transfer completes.

What happens if the connection drops?

The transfer stops and the receiver is notified. Just create a fresh link and start again.
